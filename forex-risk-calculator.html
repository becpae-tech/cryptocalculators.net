What is a Forex Risk Calculator?

A forex risk calculator is a tool to effectively manage risk when trading the forex market.

Since forex leverage increases the risk substantially, the calculator comes in handy no matter what leverage ratio you choose.

The calculator helps you decide the correct position size by recommending the appropriate position size in lots and units and also by showing you the total risk in your currency.

It helps you determine how much money to risk per trade relative to your total capital, risk in percentage, and your stop loss level in pips.

The forex risk management calculator helps you avoid overexposing yourself to risk on any single trade, which is one of the most important parts of risk management in trading.

How to Calculate Forex Risk

Calculating your risk when trading forex is an important step in preparing for a successful trade and minimizing potential loss.

  • Your total capital: Begin by assessing how much money you can allocate to each trade.
  • Figure out risk percentage per trade: Decide what percentage of your total capital you are willing to risk on a single trade (commonly 1-2%).
  • Your stop loss level: Your stop loss is what will block potential losses. Use the calculator to experiment with different stop loss levels.
  • Calculate the risk in money: Know how much you are risking per trade. Example: $5000 account, 1% risk → $50 risk per trade.
  • Selecting position size: Use the calculator to determine the appropriate position size in lots and units. Example: Risk $200 per trade, stop loss 20 pips, pip value $1 → 10 units.
Calculation Formula

The Forex Risk Calculator uses the following formula:

Position Size = (Account Size × Risk Percentage) / (Stop Loss in Pips × Pip Value)

Explanation:

  • Account Size: Total money in your trading account.
  • Risk Percentage: Portion of total capital to risk per trade.
  • Stop Loss in Pips: Maximum allowed movement against your trade.
  • Pip Value: Value of a single pip movement in the currency pair being traded. Varies by currency pair and trade size.

Always be aware of the risks involved with leverage before trading, as forex carries substantial risk.
