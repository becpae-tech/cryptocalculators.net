Frequently Asked Questions

1. Why is risk management important in crypto trading?

Risk management protects your capital from unexpected market swings. Even experienced traders can lose money without predefined stop loss levels or position size strategies. Using risk calculators can prevent large losses and improve long-term profitability.

2. How do I use a stop loss calculator?

Define the percentage of your account you are willing to risk per trade. Input entry price and account size into the stop loss calculator. It will give you the exact exit price. Try Stop Loss Calculator →

3. How do I calculate my position size?

Position size depends on your account size and acceptable risk per trade. The position size calculator converts risk into the amount of coins or contracts. Open Position Size Tool →

4. What is a good risk-reward ratio?

A common guideline is 1:2 or higher. Risk-reward calculators help plan trades. Check Risk Reward Tool →

5. How does leverage affect my trades?

Leverage amplifies profits and losses. Use a leverage calculator to avoid forced liquidation. Use Leverage Calculator →

6. What is a liquidation price?

Liquidation price is when exchange closes your position automatically. Calculate it in advance to protect your capital. Check Liquidation Calculator →

7. How do I calculate trading fees?

Trading fees, funding rates, and spreads reduce profits. Estimate costs with a fee calculator. Open Fee Calculator →

8. What is funding rate and why does it matter?

Funding rate is a payment between long/short positions. Knowing it avoids unexpected costs. Check Funding Rate Tool →

9. Are these tools suitable for beginners?

Yes. Calculators remove guesswork and help beginners trade systematically.

10. Can these tools improve my trading strategy?

Combining stop loss, position size, risk-reward, and fee calculators allows careful planning, risk management, and profitable trading.
