What is Short Selling?

Short selling is a trading strategy used to profit from a decline in an asset's price. Traders borrow an asset, sell it at the current price, and aim to buy it back at a lower price. Short Selling Calculator helps plan trades, position size, and profit potential.

Why Use a Short Selling Calculator?
  • Risk Management: Determine position size and potential loss before entering a trade.
  • Profit Estimation: Quickly calculate potential profit and % return.
  • Leverage Planning: Assess effects of leverage on your trade.
  • Professional Strategy: Works for crypto, forex, stocks, and futures.
How to Use the Short Selling Calculator

Short Selling Formula

Position Size = Sale Price × Number of Shares × Leverage (if applied)
Profit = (Sale Price − Buyback Price) × Number of Shares × Leverage (if applied)
% Return = (Profit ÷ Position Size) × 100%

Short Selling Calculator（Example）

Short 2 BTC at $50,000 with expected Buyback $45,000, using 2x leverage:
Position Size = $50,000 × 2 × 2 = $200,000
Profit = (50,000 − 45,000) × 2 × 2 = $20,000
% Return = 20,000 ÷ 200,000 × 100% = 10%
