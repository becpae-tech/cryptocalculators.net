How to use the Risk Reward Ratio Calculator
  1. Enter your Entry Price (eg. $125, or $25,000).
  2. Enter your Stop Loss Price.
  3. Enter your Take Profit Price.
  4. Click Calculate to see your Risk Reward Ratio.
  5. Click Reset to clear all fields.

Knowing your risk to reward is a critical concept for day traders to create effective strategies. The Risk Reward Ratio Calculator measures the risk for every dollar spent based on your entry price, stop loss price, and your take profit price.

Formula / Calculation

Risk: |Entry Price − Stop Loss Price|
Reward: |Take Profit Price − Entry Price|
Risk Reward Ratio: Reward / Risk

Risk Reward Ratio measures FAQ
❓ Q1: What is a risk reward ratio calculator?

A: Risk Reward Ratio measures how much potential profit you can earn compared to the potential loss. Higher is better.

A risk reward ratio calculator helps traders evaluate whether a trade is worth taking based on the potential profit versus the potential loss.

❓ Q2:What factors are important to consider when calculating the risk-to-reward ratio?

A: You want to make sure that your inputs match the true prices for your trade. This means that if you add an entry price of $250 to the calculator you need to stick to that entry price and enter at that price. The same thing goes for the stop loss and the take profit.

❓ Q3: How often should I re-calculate my risk-to-reward ratio?

A: Only reassess your risk reward ratio if your trade plan or setup has changed. If your plan stays the same, stick with the same calculations.

❓ Q4:How can I determine a good risk-reward ratio?

A:Typically, a win-loss ratio of more than 2 is always good since it lets you have a win rate of only 50% to break even.

❓ Q5: Can I use it for crypto, forex, and stocks?

A: Yes, it works for all asset types including crypto, forex, and stocks.

❓ Q6: Is this calculator free?

A: Yes, completely free and works directly in your browser.
