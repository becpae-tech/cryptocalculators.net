What is Margin Call?

A margin call occurs when your account equity falls below the required maintenance margin. This calculator determines the price at which a margin call is triggered.

Formula / Calculation

Long: P0 × (1 - Initial Margin) ÷ (1 - Maintenance Margin)
Short: P0 × (1 + Initial Margin) ÷ (1 + Maintenance Margin)

Margin Call Calculator FAQ
Q1: What is a margin call price in trading?

A: The margin call price is the price level at which a broker demands additional funds because the trader’s equity has fallen below the maintenance margin. It helps traders know when positions risk forced liquidation.

Q2: How do you calculate margin call price?

A: For a long position, it is:

Margin Call Price = Initial Price × (1 − Initial Margin %) ÷ (1 − Maintenance Margin %) For short positions:

Margin Call Price = Initial Price × (1 + Initial Margin %) ÷ (1 + Maintenance Margin %)

This formula shows how far price must move before maintenance requirements are breached.

Q3: Can I calculate margin call price for free online?

A: Yes — many free online tools provide margin call price calculation. These tools let you enter your entry price, initial and maintenance margins to get instant results without registration or cost. Make sure the tool supports both long and short positions for accuracy
