Is the calculator free & online?

Yes, this CFD Calculator is free to use and works entirely online without registration.

How to use CFD Calculator
  1. Select the position direction (long or short)
  2. Enter your Entry Price (eg. 200, 1.1154, 20000)
  3. Enter your Exit Price (eg. 44, 1.3523, 30000)
  4. Add your Margin Requirement (eg. 200, 10, 500)
  5. Select the Leverage Ratio (eg. 10, 25, 50)
  6. Click Calculate or press Enter on your keyboard

The result will show up at the bottom of the calculator.

How does it work?
  • The CFD Profit Calculator computes your gain or loss based on trade direction, entry/exit prices, margin, and leverage.
  • The CFD Margin Calculator computes the required margin to open a position based on size and leverage.
Formula / Calculation

Profit = (Exit Price - Entry Price) × Margin × Leverage (for Long) or (Entry Price - Exit Price) × Margin × Leverage (for Short)
Margin Requirement = Position Size / Leverage

CFD Calculator FAQ
Q1: What is a CFD calculator and how does it work?
  • A: A CFD calculator is a trading tool that helps you estimate the profit, loss, required margin, or leverage outcomes of a CFD trade based on entry price, exit price, lot size, and leverage settings. It simplifies complex trading math into real‑time results.
Q2: Why use a free online CFD calculator instead of manual calculation?
  • A: Manual calculations can be error prone and time‑consuming, especially when handling leverage, margin, and multiple instruments. A free online CFD calculator instantly computes results accurately and helps traders plan strategies quickly without spreadsheets.
Q3: Does a CFD calculator account for spreads and overnight funding costs?
  • A: Most basic CFD calculators focus on profit and margin without incorporating spreads or overnight funding. Advanced or broker‑specific calculators may include those costs, but it’s important to verify what is included in each tool before making decisions.
