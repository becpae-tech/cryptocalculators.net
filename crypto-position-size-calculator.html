How to calculate crypto position size

Below is a step-by-step on how to use the crypto position size calculator:

  • Add your total account size
  • Input the price of the cryptocurrency
  • Select your stop loss price

The result you are given is the total position size that you should use for any cryptocurrency you are trading to stay in line with your maximum risk profile.

The formula for calculating your position size is done by first choosing the account size (risk capital), secondly, you want to select the entry price for your cryptocurrency. Then you add the stop loss price. Finally, you add the maximum allowed risk for the position. The maximum allowed risk is always calculated on your total account value.

For example, if your account size is $2000 and you choose a maximum risk of 1%, which is $20, the calculator will give you a position size for crypto that stays within that risk limit. The calculation will of course change depending on how far away your stop loss is from your entry price.

What is a crypto position size calculator?

A crypto position size calculator is a tool that helps you calculate the right position size when trading cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin or Solana. The position size is based on your total account size, the price of the cryptocurrency, the stop loss price, and your maximum risk in percentage.
